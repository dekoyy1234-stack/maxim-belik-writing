The Best Time to Visit Bali: A Month-by-Month Guide

Travel guide · ~8 min read

Short answer: the best time to visit Bali is May, June and September — dry, sunny, and just before or after the July–August crowds. But "best" depends on what you want: cheap flights, empty beaches, or the right swell for surfing. Here's the honest month-by-month version.

Bali only really has two seasons: dry and wet. Everything else — prices, crowds, how good the sunsets are — hangs off those two. Get the timing right and you get warm mornings, cheap villas, and beaches you don't have to share. Get it wrong and you're paying peak rates to watch rain hammer the pool. Below is what each month actually feels like on the ground.

Bali's Two Seasons, in Plain Terms

The dry season runs April to October: lower humidity, reliable sun, calmer seas on the south coast. The wet season runs November to March: short, heavy afternoon downpours, greener rice terraces, and far fewer tourists. It rarely rains all day even in the wet season — but when it comes, it comes hard.

Peak, Shoulder and Low Season

Shoulder season (best value): May, June, September

This is the sweet spot most guides bury at the bottom. The weather is dry-season quality, but you dodge the July–August price spike and the crowds. Villas that cost a fortune in August are suddenly reasonable. If you can only travel once, aim here.

Peak season: July, August, and the Christmas–New Year window

Great weather, worst crowds, highest prices. Popular restaurants need bookings, Canggu traffic crawls, and the good villas sell out months ahead. Come now only if these are your fixed holiday dates — and book early.

Low season: January and February

The wettest months. The upside nobody mentions: this is when Bali is cheapest and quietest, the rice fields are impossibly green, and a rainy hour is a good excuse for a long lunch. Bring a light rain jacket and don't over-plan beach days.

Month by Month, Quickly

April — dry season begins, still uncrowded. One of the most underrated months.
May — warm, dry, quiet. Excellent all-round.
June — reliably sunny, prices still sane. Great for first-timers.
July–August — peak everything. Book ahead or skip.
September — the crowds thin, weather holds. Arguably the best single month.
October — dry season winding down, good value, occasional shower.
November–December — wet season starts; December fills up again for the holidays.
January–February — wettest and cheapest. Green, calm, and slow.
March — rain easing off; Nyepi (the island's silent day) usually falls here and shuts everything down for 24 hours — worth planning around.

If You're Coming to Surf

Surfers flip the calendar. The dry season (May–September) lights up the west-coast breaks — Uluwatu, Padang Padang, Bingin — with clean offshore winds. In the wet season the wind swings and the east coast (Nusa Dua, Keramas, Sanur) comes alive instead. So there's good surf year-round; you just move sides of the island.

So, When Should You Go?

If you want the easy answer: go in May, June or September. You get dry-season weather without peak-season prices or crowds. Want it cheap and quiet and don't mind an afternoon shower? January and February will surprise you. Fixed on July or August? It's still Bali — just book early and expect company.
